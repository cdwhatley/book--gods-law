God's Law for Christians: A Call to Holiness

Chapter 5: Wasn’t God’s Law Nailed To The Cross?

But, wasn’t God’s Law nailed to the Cross making it null and void? If so, the Law is no longer valid for either physical or spiritual Israel.

“15There will be one Law for you and for the stranger who stays with you. It will be a Law forever for all your children-to-come. As you are, so will the stranger be before the Lord. 16One Law will be for you and for the stranger who stays with you.”
 Numbers 15:16 (NLV)

Was God lying in Numbers 15:16 when he said that His Law is forever?

God even made the point to remind us about His Law being forever when giving specific instructions in the Torah.

The Weekly Sabbath

“15Work shall be done for six days, but the seventh is the Sabbath of rest, holy to the LORD. Whoever does any work on the Sabbath day, he shall surely be put to death. 16Therefore the children of Israel shall keep the Sabbath, to observe the Sabbath throughout their generations as a perpetual covenant. 17It is a sign between Me and the children of Israel forever; for in six days the LORD made the heavens and the earth, and on the seventh day He rested and was refreshed.”
 Exodus 31:15-17 (NKJV)

Passover

“So this day shall be to you a memorial; and you shall keep it as a feast to the LORD throughout your generations. You shall keep it as a feast by an everlasting ordinance.”
 Exodus 12:14 (NKJV)
“And you shall observe this thing as an ordinance for you and your sons forever.”
 Exodus 12:24 (NKJV)

Jesus even clearly stated that He did not come to Earth to end God’s Law. Instead He came to fulfill it.

“17Don’t think that I came to abolish the Law or the Prophets. I did not come to abolish but to fulfill. 18For truly I tell you, until heaven and earth pass away, not the smallest letter or one stroke of a letter will pass away from the Law until all things are accomplished. 19Therefore, whoever breaks one of the least of these commands and teaches others to do the same will be called least in the kingdom of heaven. But whoever does and teaches these commands will be called great in the kingdom of heaven.”
 Matthew 5:17-19 (CSB)

In Matthew 5:17, “abolish” is the Greek word “kataluo” (Strong G2647). The word means “to demolish, destroy, throw down, overthrow”.

“Fulfill” is the Greek word “pleroo” (Strongs G4137). Pleroo could mean “to make full” or it could mean “finish a period or task; to accomplish; to end”. The meaning depends on how the word is used in the passage. Traditional Christianity has interpreted “pleroo” here to mean “finish, accomplish, end”.

But if that is indeed the correct interpretation, then Jesus’ own words in Matthew 5:17 contradict themselves. In essence He would have been saying, “I did not come to abolish (destroy or overthrow) the Law, but to finish/end it”. I think we can all agree if something is destroyed or overthrown, it is no longer used, effectively ending its life. So Jesus would be saying He didn’t come to end the Law but to end the Law.

The meaning of “pleroo” that matches the context of what Jesus said is “to make full”. This could mean to make full the understanding of the Law, in essence filling it up with meaning. That makes sense when looking at the many points Jesus made in that same conversation, known as the Sermon on the Mount. For instance, he stated that the law to not commit adultery has a fuller meaning than just the physical act. He said that if a man even looks at a woman in lust, he has broken the commandment.

Jesus goes on to say that not even the smallest letter or marking in the Torah would be made invalid until Heaven and Earth pass away and all things are accomplished. The fact that we are still here on Earth means God’s Law is still valid.

5.1 So What Was Nailed To The Cross?

When a person breaks a governmental law (law of the land), there is a penalty to be paid. It’s the same with God’s Law. When any person breaks God’s Law (i.e. commits a sin), there is a penalty that must be paid. Each person is responsible for paying his/her own penalty. Ultimately that penalty for breaking God’s Law is eternal separation from God, i.e. death.

The wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.
 Romans 6:23 (KJV)
Fathers are not to be put to death for children, and children are not to be put to death for fathers—each one is to be put to death for his own sin.
 Deuteronomy 14:16 (TLV)

It was that penalty for our sins (for our breaking of God’s Law) that was nailed to the cross, not God’s Law itself, as stated in Colossians 2.

13You were dead because you were sinful and were not God’s people. But God let Christ make you alive, when He forgave all our sins 14God wiped out the charges that were against us for disobeying the Law of Moses. He took them away and nailed them to the cross.
 Colossians 2:13-14 (CEV)
