God's Law for Christians: A Call to Holiness

Chapter 10: Christian Arguments Against Obeying God's Law

The following are some of the doctrines that are taught in Christian churches about God's Law.

  1. The Law has been done away with (voided/canceled/no longer applies).
  2. The Law was nailed to the Cross.
  3. The Law is a burden.
  4. Following the Law puts believers under bondage.
  5. The Law was fulfilled by Jesus so we don't have to follow it.
  6. We are under grace, not the Law. The New Covenant is a covenant of grace; the Old Covenant is a covenant of law.
  7. Christianity is not about following rules (the Law); it’s about a relationship with God/Jesus.
  8. Only the moral law of the Mosaic Law applies to Christians; the ceremonial and ritual laws have been canceled.
  9. Dual-Covenant Theology: Mosaic Covenant only remains valid for Jews; Gentiles are not under the Law
  10. The 10 Commandments still apply for Christians, but only those that are repeated as commands in the New Testament. Since the Sabbath command is not repeated, the Sabbath law (4th law) does not apply.

10.1 Argument #1: God's Law Has Been Done Away With

Supporting Scriptures

For sin shall not be master over you, for you are no longer under law but under grace..
 Romans 6:14 (TLV)
1Or do you not know, brothers and sisters (for I speak to those who know law), that the law is master over a person as long as he lives? 2For the married woamn is bound by law to her husband while he lives ,but if the husband dies, she is released from the law concerning her husband. 3So then, if she is joined to another man while her husband is living, she will be called an adulteress, though she is joined to another man. 4Therefore, my brothers and sisters, you also were made dead to the Torah through the body of Messiah, so that you might be joined to another - the One who was raised from the dead - in order that we might bear fruit for God. 5For when we were in the flesh, the sinful passions that came through the Torah were working in our body parts to bear fruit for death. 6But now we have been released from the law, having died to what confined us, so that we serve in the new way of the Ruach [Spirit] and not in the old way of the letter..
 Romans 7:1-6 (TLV)

Traditional Interpretation

Christians are not subject to obedience to God's Law.

Counter Scriptures

If the Law is not sinful and the Law defines what sin is, why would Christians not try to obey the Law? Jesus said on more than one occasion to people, “Go and sin no more” (John 5:14 and John 8:11).

He [Yeshua] said to him,
“Look, you've been healed! Stop sinning, so nothing worse happes to you.”
 John 5:14 (TLV)
Yeshua said,
“Go and sin no more.”
 John 8:11 (TLV)

Jesus also said:

17Do not think that I came to abolish the Torah or the Prophets! I did not come to abolish, but to fulfill. 18Amen, I tell you, until heaven and earth pass away, not the smallest letter or serif shall ever pass away from the Torah until all things come to pass. 19Therefore, whoever breaks one of the least of these commandments, and teaches others the same, shall be called least in the kingdom of heaven. But whoever keeps and teaches them, this one shall be called great in the kingdom of heaven.
 Matthew 5:17-19 (TLV)

Paul said:

14For we know that the Torah is spiritual; but I am of the flesh, sold to sin. 15For I do not understand what I am doing – for what I do not want, this I practice; but what I hate, this I do. 16But if I do what I do not want to do, then I agree with the Torah – that it is good.
 Romans 7:14-16 (TLV)
22For I delight in the Torah of God with respect to the inner man, 23but I see a different law in my body parts, battling against the law of my mind and bringing me into bondage under the law of sin which is in my body parts.
 Romans 7:22-23 (TLV)
... So then, with my mind I myself serve the Torah of God; but with my flesh, I serve the law of sin.
 Romans 7:25 (TLV)
Do we then nullify the Torah through faithfulness? May it never be! On the contrary, we uphold the Torah.
 Romans 3:31 (TLV)

Counter Interpretation

The law has not been made void for Christians but still applies. Jesus tells us to not sin (i.e, break God’s Law) and that God’s Law will not pass away until heaven and earth pass away. Paul says that he strives to serve/obey God’s Law. He also says that we do not make void the Torah through our faith in Messiah, but rather we uphold (keep) the Torah.
