God's Law for Christians: A Call to Holiness

Chapter 15: Traditional Doctrine: New Covenant Believers Are Led By The Spirit, Not By The Law

Supporting Scriptures

16I say then, walk by the Spirit and you will not carry out the desire of the flesh. 17For the flesh desires what is against the Spirit, and the Spirit desires what is against the flesh; these are opposed to each other, so that you don’t do what you want. 18But if you are led by the Spirit, you are not under the law.
 Galatians 5:16-18 (HCSB)
But now we have been released from the law, since we have died to what held us, so that we may serve in the new way of the Spirit and not in the old letter of the law.
 Romans 7:6 (HCSB)

Traditional Interpretation

Today, under the New Covenant, believers are led by the Holy Spirit (traditionally referred to as "walking by the Spirit"). This means the Mosaic Law is not used to guide our lives, but rather the speaking and impressions of the Holy Spirit to our spirit. Under the Old Covenant, believers were led by the Law.

Counter Scriptures

5For those who live according to the flesh think about the things of the flesh, but those who live according to the Spirit, about the things of the Spirit. 6For the mind-set of the flesh is death, but the mind-set of the Spirit is life and peace. 7For the mind-set of the flesh is hostile to God because it does not submit itself to God’s law, for it is unable to do so. 8Those who are in the flesh cannot please God. 9You, however, are not in the flesh, but in the Spirit, since the Spirit of God lives in you.
Romans 8:5-9 (HCSB)
For we know that the law is spiritual, but I am made out of flesh, sold into sin’s power.
 Romans 8:14 (HCSB)

Counter Interpretation

The opposing factions are not the Holy Spirit and God's Law, but instead our flesh (i.e., our physical desires) and the Holy Spirit. God's Law is spiritual; our flesh, however, is not. The Holy Spirit and God's Law are completely aligned. If we follow the Holy Spirit instead of our physical desires, we will follow God's Law. If we follow our flesh, we will not follow God's Law. As Paul states, "the flesh is hostile to God because it does not submit itself to God's Law".
